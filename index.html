Selected projects

surface attachment

Bacteria segmentation-watershed algorithm

The algorithm treats an image as a topological surface where the pixel intensity corresponds to the area height. It then identifies the edge of the cell as the watershed separating neighboring drainage basins. The limit of watershed algorithm grows based on the background intensity. Each cell need a individual seed which is selected by a lower threshold. Outer boundaries of cells are set by a higher threshold.

Cell shape model-PSICIC algorithm

The shape of bacteria is modeled by PSICIC algorithm, which calculates the length of a bending cell. Briefly, the algorithm finds two poles of a cell as points that are the greatest Euclidean distance apart, thus creating two curves. On each of the two contour curves the algorithm evenly distributes equal number of points and then connects them (effectively creating width lines). Finally, the center line, i.e. the length of the cell, runs along the middle of the width line.

Flat cell detection-HOG descriptor and SVM classifier

Bacteria are adhered on the coverslip by different attachments for the observation. However, some of the cells are not fully attached on the 2D surface, and the tilt would cause uncertainty on the following analysis. As a result, a system is developed to filter out cells which are not flat. First, segmented cells are characterized by the histogram of gradient (HOG) feature descriptor. Then, the descriptors is fed into SVM classifier. The SVM classifier is trained by 295 labeled data, and the accuracy turns out to be 0.92. The white circle points out the non flat cell which is filtered out by this work.

Bacteria tracking-mean shift algorithm

Tracking of swimming bacteria under the 2D field of view of microscope is implemented by the mean shift algorithm in IDL language. The mean shift algorithm is a so-call mode-seeking algorithm which finds the maxima of a density function. The target cell is located by a small window, and the algorithm keeps moving the window to find the possible location of the cell across time frames. The figure shows tracking trajectories of different bacteria, and red dots indicate turning points.

Spiral-coil formation

Bacteria collective motion simulation-molecular dynamics simulation

The 2D collective motion of one ultra long cell and several short cells is simulated by a coarse-grained Brownian dynamics method, and this simulation is programmed in C language. Bacteria in our system are composed of several connected self-propelled, steric beads. Each chain of beads is regarded as a semi-flexible active polymer in low Reynolds number fluid. The calculation is speed up by linked cell method which is suit for the rapid decay potential.

Bacteria clustering-DBSCAN algorithm

Clusters of short bacteria collective motion are separated by this work. DBSCAN is a clustering algorithm which is based on local density and allows the existence of noise points. The cluster identification in our system is a spatio-temporal problem, self-propelled chains gather together in space, and move toward the same direction. DBSCAN plays the role of spatial clustering method. Besides, the temporal clustering is characterized by setting the maximum angle deviation of the velocity between neighbor chains as an additional criteria.
